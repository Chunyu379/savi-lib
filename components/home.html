Located North West of downtown Dallas, The Salvation Army Carr P. Collins Social Service Center is next to Parkland Hospital on Harry Hines Boulevard.

The 161,000 square foot center, which houses up to 600 people per night, is the largest multi-use facility in The Salvation Army world. Every year, thousands of people find the spiritual and practical help they need to change their lives at The Salvation Army Carr P. Collins Social Service Center. Nowhere else in Dallas will people find so many resources under one roof.

Programs & Services

Substance Abuse Treatment Program

The Domestic Violence Program offers safe shelter in a confidential location for battered women and their children. The program utilizes a trauma informed approach to service delivery, which includes safety planning, children's programming, individual and group therapy, legal advocacy, and case management.

Veterans Programs

The Carr P. Collins Center offers temporary housing and supportive services for male and female veterans experiencing homelessness. Veterans who are other than dishonorably discharged are screened and referred for placement by the Veteran's Administration. The program can serve 110 veterans at a given time, and they can stay for up to 2 years. The overall goal is to provide a safe stable residential environment that meets their basic needs, while assisting the veterans to achieve self-sufficiency.

Emergency Shelter

The Carr P. Collins Center's emergency shelter provides men, women, and women with children who are experiencing homelessness a safe refuge from the streets with sleeping accommodations, two hot meals a day, showers, clothes, and use of laundry facilities. Through the emergency shelter, clients have access to longer term programming and additional services that address immediate barriers to stability.

Transitional Shelter

Thrive and Step-up transitional shelter programs serve men, women, and women with children who are experiencing homelessness. The Thrive program addresses the needs of those already working or work-ready. The Step-up Program addresses the needs of those who are disabled due to mental illness, medical conditions, and/or substance abuse. The goals are to address barriers to stability, assist clients to successfully transition to permanent housing, and provide clients the tools and resources so they can demonstrate housing stability six months after placement.

Domestic Violence Program (Confidential Location)

The Domestic Violence Program offers safe shelter in a confidential location for battered women and their children. The program utilizes a trauma informed approach to service delivery, which includes safety planning, children's programming, individual and group therapy, legal advocacy, and case management.

Home Sweet Home

The Home Sweet Home Program uses a combination of classroom and one-on-one client centered financial education coupled with limited direct financial assistance in order to prevent homelessness and support residential stability for at-risk households. The goal is that through financial education, clients are empowered with the knowledge to effectively manage their finances to achieve and maintain residential stability.

Worship Programs

The Salvation Army is an evangelical part of the universal Christian church. It's message is based on the Bible. It's ministry is motivated by the love of God. It's mission is to preach the Gospel of Jesus Christ and to meet human needs in His name without discrimination. The Car P. Collins Center offers mainstream Christian teaching at Sunday School and worship services on Sunday, as well as Bible study and fellowship groups during the week.

Life-Skills Classes

Life-skills classes are an essential component of each of our programs and are designed to provide valuable education and information on the tools that will help our clients be successful as they move towards stability and independence. Topics for life-skills classes include resume building, interview skills, health and nutrition, relapse prevention, coping skills and parenting.

Food Pantry

The Carr P. Collins Center distributes over 800 bags of groceries a month to community members in need. Households receive one bag per adult and additional bags for children based on family size. They are eligible to receive food pantry services once every 30 days.

Meals

Between 1,200 and 1,500 meals are served to clients at this center each day. Sack lunches are provided for clients going to work or for other community members needing a meal. At Thanksgiving and Christmas, the center opens its doors to the community, offering a holiday feast to those individuals and families who may not otherwise have the opportunity.

Summer Heat Relief - Cooling Station

Extreme heat can be life-threatening. During the summer months, we provide an air-conditioned sitting area and water to keep people hydrated.
